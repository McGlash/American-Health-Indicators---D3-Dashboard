Health Indicators of Americans

The American Community Survey (ACS) is an ongoing survey of Americans. The following analysis is based on 2014 ASC estimates.

The following interactive chart shows health indicators in relation to demographics by state. Click on axes labels to change views.

Relationships Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Financial Health

Besides lack of healthcare insurance, the prominence of smoking and obesity appear to depend on financial health. Rates are higher in states with lower median incomes. Similarly, rates are higher in states with more residents living in poverty.

Smoking Aging Out

Older States tend to have higher rates of smoking.

Age and Access

Younger States tend to have higher proportions of residents without healthcare insurance.
